In a linked list with a cycle, the fast and slow pointers will eventually meet. Here's a brief explanation of how it works:

Initialization: Both pointers start at the head of the linked list.
Movement: The slow pointer moves one step at a time, while the fast pointer moves two steps at a time.
Meeting Point: If there is a cycle, the fast pointer will eventually catch up to the slow pointer within the cycle.
