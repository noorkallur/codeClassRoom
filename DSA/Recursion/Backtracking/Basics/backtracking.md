# BackTracking
Backtracking is a general algorithmic technique used for solving problems incrementally by trying partial solutions and then abandoning them if they do not lead to a valid solution. It's often used in problems involving combinatorics, constraint satisfaction, and search, such as:

Solving mazes
N-Queens problem
Sudoku solver
Generating permutations or combinations
